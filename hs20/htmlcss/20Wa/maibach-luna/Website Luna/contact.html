LET'S GO

A letter is a piece you can hold in your hand. It is a carefully coordinated collection of envelopes, letterheads, stamps and contents. The recipient can read his or her name on the envelope in the handwriting of the loved or beloved sender. After reading it several times, it is carefully stored like a treasure and can be erased as a memory after many years. No hard drive crash can damage it. For many problems, the old form of communication of letters has more style than emails, no matter how many smileys and GIF graphics there are.
